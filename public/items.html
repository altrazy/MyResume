My goal

  • I want to help the team become more successful and efficient which will help the overall branch become more successful and efficient.
  • I'd like get it to where everyone is on a similar skill level in their respective tech position so that when someone asks for a tech they don’t have to worry about whether or not they'll be able to achieve what is asked of them.

More Regular Self Evaluations/ One on one meetings

  • Self-evaluations shouldn't be limited to just once a year.
  • People are more prone to follow through with a plan/idea if they come up with it on their own. (learned from being a crisis counselor volunteer.)
    • Have them assess their performance on the job they are on or a past job.
    • During the self-evaluations have them create a idea on how to improve areas they identified
    • We will then talk together on how to create a SMART Goal to achieve their plan.
      Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-Bound
    • Follow up monthly to see how they are coming along and encourage them to reach out if they need help
    • This will help them achieve their yearly evaluations and goals.
    • Overall them evaluating themselves would not only help their growth but the branch aswell.
  • Giving them quarterly (or more often) tests to assess their knowledge and progress.
    • These could cover various areas like CCT, SCT, Wiring details, Checkout procedure, worksites like selection navigator so they know how to get drawings and upload documents.
    • these tests would provide a understanding of their knowledge in each subject, allowing us to identify areas for improvement and provide targeted support

Items I'd like to address:

  • Close out Items that they are responsible for
    • Checkoff sheets
      • Uniformity
        • Have them all complete checkout the same way with the same tool.
    • Expectations for what needs to be on them
    • Have them send them in from time to time to ensure they follow what is expected
    • As-Builts/Red lines
      • Ensure they understand what is expected of them when on site
    • Final SCT database upload with Caf files

  • When a LSS directs them or asks for someone
    • They need to provide the tech with the MC or subs number to call and ensure the site is ready for them to do the task needed
      • Many techs have said they've shown up and what they were meant to do isn't ready for them. This is wasted time.
    • If they want to reach out to the tech to let them know where they need to go instead of the STL they need to CC the STL.
      • If we don’t see them reach out before 3pm we will reach out in their place ccing them.
      • This will prevent the tech from not being informed and showing up to the wrong place.

  • During weekly meetings ask the following:
    1. What is going well in our team?
    2. Where can we improve?
    3. How can I help?
      • My role is to help this team grow and to ensure we're profittable. Both items go hand in hand.
      • I might be blind to something obvious to them
    4. Where do I need to improve?
      • Make it anonymous so people aren't scared of repercussions
      • Reflect on the feedback and talk to the team about it at the next meeting.
    5. Taking time to Celebrate the wins.
      • Any meaning no matter how small drives human motivation.
      • Feeling meaning is like working out a muscle if you don’t do it often it will start to shrink.
      • People want more than just money. If we can make them feel meaning and understand the impact they have then they might not always ask for a pay raise.
      • Create a team page to share wins in
    • This will help us to see blind spots for improvement
    • Getting them involved with changes will help them see value and keep them engaged

  • Promoting an open door culture
    • Being approachable and building trust are important to leading people
    • If you are approachable then people are more likely to reach out or come to you when they have an issue instead of hiding it until it inevitably comes to light

  • Create more structure to help everyone
    • Using this framework we can create a baseline for each tier of technician to help evaluate where everyone is at and who needs help
      • Using the baseline for hiring employees and letting them know what is expected of them after a year
      • This will help to create criteria for promotions to tech 2 and tech 3.
      • Communicating this criteria/baseline with them techs will have something to strive for.
      • Even if there are limited spots for each position transparency is key
      • Leaving them in the dark can be discouraging and may lead to turnover of good talent.
