Items I'd focus on implementing at my level if I were a Leader.

More regular Self evaluations/one on one meetings

I believe that self-evaluations shouldn't be limited to just once a year. Both employees and the company could benefit from having individuals conduct self-assessments after completing a project. While it may not be necessary for every project, perhaps conducting evaluations quarterly could be beneficial. This approach could help individuals recognize or evaluate their strengths and weaknesses more regularly, Ultimately leading to their growth in the areas they are lacking in. Additionally, reflecting on the work they've done could help them see the value they add to Johnson Controls.
  • After they conduct a Self Evaluations and had time to really go through it we can sit down with them and talk about it going over the following.
    • Where there things they feel they did really good at?
      Depending on how big of an ackomplisment it was Recognize them for it during a team meeting. Or at the very least give them some sort of praise during the one on one. As a leader you should be building your team up. A little praise such as "man you did an awesome job." could go a long way.
    • Where there areas they Felt they struggled in?
      Talk with them about it and come up with a plan or solution to develop them so next time they'll be and feel more successful. We're here to build them up this isn't a time to berate them. If the people below you dont trust you they wont come to you when they are in need of help.
    • Where there things they had no Idea what they were doing or what was expected of them?
      This is similar to my thoughts above. We're here to assist them if we send them to a site to perform a task and they aren't prepared or how no idea how to do it that directly reflects on us.
  • Ultimately I think this would not just help their growth but also help our branch

Quarterly one on ones

These one on ones could be longer than the monthly one on ones are and used to cover the following:
  • Tracking and discussing their areas of improvement.
  • Setting SMART goals with them
    talk with them about where they think they need to improve and use the SMART format to ensure it is something obtainable in a quater. Each month follow up during those one on ones to see how their progress is coming along. If they achieved their goal maybe get with them to set a new one.
  • Review their performance on the projects they've been working on with them.
  • Giving them quarterly tests to assess their knowledge and progress. These could cover various areas like CCT, SCT, Wiring details, Checkout procedure, worksites like selection navigator so they know how to get drawings and upload documents.
  • these tests would provide a understanding of their knowledge in each subject, allowing us to identify areas for improvement and provide targeted support
